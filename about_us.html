College Goals:

The college shall provide academic excellence in the field of Information and Communication Technology, with emphasis on the following goals:

  1. Produce quality, excellent and environmentally proactive graduates imbued with gender responsiveness.
  2. Achieve the highest level of accreditation and center of excellence imbued with outcomes-based education.
  3. Partner with national and international industries as an outlet for research development and exten
  4. Support faculty members through faculty development programs to be competitive with the highest global standards.

Under Graduate Programs Offered:

BACHELOR OF SCIENCE IN COMPUTER SCIENCE (BSCS), CHED IX Order No. 001, Series of 2014
Program Objectives/Outcomes
  • Utilize effectively the concepts of computer science theories and methodologies and adapt new technologies and ideas in formulating effective solutions to address public health and safety, cultural, societal, gender and environmental considerations. (Competent Computer Science Professional, Socially Responsive)
  • Work cohesively with members of a team using their individual skills to the successful completion of a project. (Team Player)
  • Pursue personal development and lifelong learning through research, graduate studies, training and membership to professional organization to be globally competitive. (Lifelong Learner)
  • Communicate effectively with the computing community and society through oral and written correspondence. (Effective Communicator)
BACHELOR OF SCIENCE IN INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY (BSIT), CHED IX Order No. 02, Series of 2017
Program Objectives/Outcomes
  • Utilizes the concepts on computing theories and methodologies, and mathematical concepts appropriate to the discipline.
  • Adapts new technologies and ideas in the design, analysis, and implementation of IT-based solutions.
  • Formulates effective solutions to solve complex computing problems that meet the desired needs and requirements under various constraints through proper research and understanding of the problem.
  • Functions effectively as a member or leader of a development team recognizing the different roles within a team for the successful completion of a project.
  • Utilizes available technical resources in formulating solutions to computing problems efficiently.
  • Analyzes the impact of information technology and its ethical and social issues on individuals and society.
  • Produces IT-related research relevant to the concerns of the community locally and globally.
  • Integrates IT-based projects for the improvement and development of society.
  • Engages in professional growth and development through self-learning, training, and postgraduate studies relevant to the field of study.

Associate Graduate Programs Offered:

Associate in Computer Technology (ACT)

  • MAJOR IN APPLICATION DEVELOPMENT, COPC NO. 70 S. 2023
  • MAJOR IN NETWORKING, COPC NO. 6, S. 2023
Program Objectives/Outcomes
  • Develop strong programming skills and the ability to design, implement, and debug computer programs using various programming languages and technologies.
  • Gain knowledge of the principles and techniques used in analyzing, designing, and documenting computer-based systems to meet user requirements effectively.
  • Understand the fundamental concepts of computer networking, including network architecture, protocols, and security, and demonstrate the ability to configure and troubleshoot network connections.
  • Acquire skills in designing and managing databases, including data modeling, querying, and administration, using appropriate database management systems.
  • Design and develop dynamic and interactive websites using web technologies such as HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and server-side scripting languages, with a focus on usability and accessibility.
  • Understand the principles of information security, including risk assessment, threat identification, and mitigation, and be able to implement security measures to protect data and systems.
  • Diagnose and resolve hardware and software issues, perform system maintenance, and troubleshoot common computer problems effectively.
  • Acquire skills in developing mobile applications for various platforms, such as Android and iOS, using relevant programming languages and frameworks.
  • Develop effective communication and teamwork skills, enabling the ability to work collaboratively, present technical information, and interact professionally with clients and colleagues.

Graduate Programs Offered:

MASTER IN INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY (MIT), COPC No. 017, Series of 2019
Program Objectives/Outcomes
  • Demonstrate advanced IT knowledge and skills in a specialized, interdisciplinary, or multidisciplinary field of learning for professional practice.
  • Utilize effectively advanced knowledge and skills in Information Technology through research and software application development that address IT-related problems of the organization and recognize gender responsiveness.
  • Apply a significant level of expertise-based autonomy and accountability to professional leadership for innovation and research in a specialized, interdisciplinary, or multidisciplinary field.
  • Pursue lifelong learning through research with a highly substantial degree of independence in individual work or teams of interdisciplinary or multidisciplinary settings.
